The 9 Best E-Commerce Podcasts You Must Follow in 2021

Conversion Rate Optimization

If you ever searched for the best e-commerce podcasts on Spotify or Apple Music, you’ve seen how many options there are out there.

As of writing, Spotify has over 2.2 million podcasts with a good handful of them around the topic of e-commerce, and they come in all shapes and forms.

Some e-commerce podcasts are geared towards experts and cover complex subjects, whereas many others target beginners and discuss the basics of e-commerce marketing.

To help you save time and energy, I’ve hand-picked nine of the best e-commerce podcasts you should listen to in 2021.

1. The eCommerce Fuel Podcast

The eCommerce fuel podcast is a big player in the industry for a reason. Andrew Youderian, the podcast host, has a long history with e-commerce, dating back to 2007 when he launched his first c-commerce store selling CB radios.

The podcast covers all e-commerce topics, including brand-specific reports and current e-commerce trends. There are over 400 episodes and 3 million total listens. 

In addition to the podcast, eCommerce Fuel also has a community for e-tailers looking for extra support. Plus, they offer a business development program that helps e-commerce owners build partnerships and grow their online stores.

Ideal for: All listener types can enjoy this podcast. However, it’s good to have a basic understanding of e-commerce before tuning in.

2. 2x eCommerce Podcast

The 2x eCommerce Podcast is hosted by the e-commerce expert Kunle Campbell and is geared towards inspiring entrepreneurs and marketers in the retail industry. 

You should check out this podcast if you’re interested in learning from various entrepreneurs, c-level executives, and marketers with a proven track record.

Each guest on the podcast shares how they doubled their e-commerce business and offers unique advice on how you can get started with your growth strategy in easy and digestible steps.  

2X podcast also hosts a Facebook community for people wanting to connect with like-minded entrepreneurs who’ve grown their online store to a seven or eight-figure business.

Ideal for: Intermediate to advanced knowledge. The language can be technical, so a deep understanding of e-commerce topics is required.

3. My Wife Quit Her Job 

If you’re thinking of starting an online business, The My Wife Quit Her Job podcast is for you. 

The host, Steve Chu, explains how his wife transitioned from a boring desk job to a successful online business. You’ll listen to a series of personal stories from entrepreneurs who are in the same boat as Steve’s wife. 

This podcast differs from others because Steve is never afraid to ask the hard questions to understand the real strategies and details behind success. 

My Wife Quit Her Job is an excellent option for beginners of e-commerce. (It might even convince you to quit your office job and start an e-commerce business.)

Ideal for: My Wife Quit Her Job covers everything from the very basics to more complex e-commerce topics. Beginners can easily understand the language and follow along, and experts can enjoy the podcast, too.

4. E-Commerce Boost

Sleeknote’s own E-Commerce Boost Podcast reviews some of the staple e-commerce marketing strategies for every industry. 

If you’re struggling with cart abandonment, SEO, or email marketing, E-Commerce Boost can help you learn about these hyper-focused areas in e-commerce.

Plus, there are no long introductions, ads, or nonsense, making it easy to learn something new in just about 20 minutes. The interviews include e-commerce gurus like Dennis Yu, Roland Pokornyik, Julie Joyce, and more. 

Ideal for: E-Commerce Boost is best for people who have basic industry knowledge. Knowing the basics will help you apply the marketing strategies discussed to your e-commerce store.

5. Future Commerce Podcast

Keeping up with future trends is crucial for your e-commerce business, and the Future Commerce Podcast can help you with that. 

Every week, experts and startup founders join the hosts, Phillip Jackson and Brian Lange, to chat about where e-commerce is going. 

It’s a podcast for innovators and founders who aren’t looking for the next conversion optimization tip or how to execute a playbook. It’s for those who want to learn something deeper than CRO and PPC. 

The podcast covers topics like new and upcoming e-commerce integrations, apps, automation tools, e-commerce statistics, and more. 

Ideal for: To listen to this podcast, you should have an intermediate to advanced understanding of e-commerce topics.

6. eCommerce FastLane

The eCommerce FastLane podcast aims to help e-commerce owners accelerate their business’s growth (specifically with Shopify). 

The podcast is hosted by Steve Hutt, the startup founder and senior Merchant Success Manager at Shopify Plus. 

Each episode interviews one of Shopify’s most successful e-commerce merchants about their marketing techniques and advice on how to grow an online store. Topics cover boutique e-commerce growth, subscriptions, recruiting, managing, and growing sales.

Ideal for: All listeners from different knowledge levels can easily follow along with the eCommerce FastLane podcast. 

7. eCommerce Lifestyle

The eCommerce Lifestyle podcast aims at helping people find freedom through entrepreneurship. The podcast talks about the current global events, helping e-commerce owners know how to manage certain situations that affect their business. 

The host, Anton Kraly, gets creative with his topics by asking unconventional questions, such as whether you should pay a supplier beforehand or not, the worst parts of dropshipping, things that will get you banned from Google, and so on. 

This podcast’s topics can help fill in knowledge gaps you can use to grow your online business.

eCommerce Lifestyle also offers courses on specific areas, including Google ads for e-commerce, inbox funnels, dropshipping, and Facebook. 

Ideal for: The eCommerce Lifestyle is a great choice for every listener. It’s easy to follow along and covers topics that affect your e-commerce store, whether you’re a beginner or not. 

8. E-commerce Conversations by Practical E-commerce

The Ecommerce Conversations by Practical Ecommerce podcast is an excellent resource for e-commerce entrepreneurs looking to gain knowledge in leadership, management skills, product procurement, and placement. 

If you enjoy hearing about what worked for other brands, this podcast is for you. Each week, Ecommerce Conversations release an episode hosted by Eric Bandholz, the founder and CEO of Beardbrand. They interview inspirational entrepreneurs and executives on the realities of online selling. Topics include finding success on Amazon, influencer marketing, list-building tools, and more.

Ideal for: The Ecommerce Conversations podcast covers topics that are valuable to every listener’s knowledge level. It’s the perfect podcast to help inspire people to start an e-commerce store or find new ways to grow an existing one.

9. The Fizzle Show

The Fizzle Show is one of the oldest podcasts in the industry, dating back to 2013 when the first episode launched. They are now one of the biggest podcasts on the market, with 5 million downloads. 

Entrepreneurs can tune in to this podcast for actionable business advice and tips from various industry experts. Podcast topics cover everything from specific tools to help your online business, success stories, life as a solo entrepreneur, and financial independence. 

While most podcast episodes are free to listen to, you can become a Fizzle member to access a robust online community dedicated to helping entrepreneurs reach their business goals. 

The membership also offers hours of training by Pat Flynn from Smart Passive Income and Dana Shultz from Minimalist Baker. There are also courses, weekly coaching sessions, and access to a Facebook community. 

Ideal for: This podcast is aimed at listeners who are hungry to grow their business, whether you’re a new e-commerce owner looking to expand your knowledge or an experienced entrepreneur looking to boost a specific part of your business.

Conclusion

Podcasts are an excellent tool for entrepreneurs to learn about new strategies and trends related to the e-commerce industry, no matter your knowledge level. 

That being said, there are thousands of e-commerce podcasts out there about every topic imaginable. I hope this list helped you narrow down to which one is right for you.
