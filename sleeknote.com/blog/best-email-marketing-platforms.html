The Best Email Marketing Platforms for E-Commerce Stores

Conversion Rate Optimization

There were 3.93 billion email users worldwide in 2019. And that number is expected to rise to over 4.48 billion by 2024.

That sheer volume coupled with the fact that “email is the preferred promotion channel for 60 percent of consumers” means it’s still an absolute must for most of today’s e-commerce brands.

The only problem is that it’s a heavily saturated market, and there are a ton of big-name email marketing platforms out there. 

So, figuring out which one to use can feel a little overwhelming, especially for newer marketers who may not understand the subtle differences between these companies.

For this post, I’m going to break it down and highlight what I feel are currently the best email marketing platforms, as well as their top features. I’ll also include plenty of screenshots so you can get a better feel of what it’s like to use each of the platforms.

When I’m done, you should have a clear idea of what distinguishes each platform from its competitors and which one is best for you based on your brand’s needs.

Here we go.

1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp is one of the “OG” email marketing platforms and was launched way back in 2001. 

One of its biggest selling points is its simplicity, which makes it perfect for beginners.

It’s easy to get started, and you can quickly take advantage of Mailchimp’s robust features like signup forms, landing pages, social posts, lookalike audience finder, and more. 

Having stunning visuals is a big part of running a successful email marketing campaign, and Mailchimp offers several design tools that can help you with this, even if you have minimal artistic chops.

There are email automations, a drag-and-drop builder, and a content studio, to name a few features.

You can choose from more than 100 pre-built templates that will fit nearly any e-commerce marketing message you can imagine. 

Mailchimp offers real-time analytics so you can always stay up-to-date on how your campaign is performing and gain insights into specific changes you need to make. 

You can also use this platform to automate a few different parts of your email marketing. For instance, you can “set up a welcome series for new subscribers or a reminder email when people leave stuff behind in their online cart.” So, there’s a lot that Mailchimp has to offer.

If you’re brand new to email marketing, this is definitely a platform to consider, and it can help you get started out on the right foot. 

2. Constant Contact 

Founded in 1995, this is another company that’s been around for a long time. Over the years, they’ve built a strong reputation for providing a quality product that’s highly customizable and user-friendly. 

For starters, Constant Contact allows you to “choose from 100+ mobile-optimized, easy-to-customize email templates that will make your brand break through the clutter.” 

Next, they have a comprehensive list of features that help you create compelling emails that look great on any device regardless of the screen size. 

There’s drag-and-drop editing.

You can easily customize your emails using a wide array of colors and fonts that mesh with your brand identity.

You can also seamlessly incorporate images into your content to create strong visuals that will resonate with subscribers.

Besides that, Constant Contact has a cool feature that allows you to easily insert products directly from your store along with buttons to buy, as well as surveys.

And like Mailchimp, Constant Contact can also help you automate your campaign. For example, you can automatically set it to send a welcome email to new subscribers to build instant rapport. Or, you can create an automated email series to nurture leads and supply them with targeted content at the most opportune times. 

If you’re looking for an email marketing platform with all of the bells and whistles but want to keep it simple, I highly recommend Constant Contact. 

3. Campaign Monitor

Campaign Monitor was launched in 2004 and has built up a strong reputation as a leading email marketing and e-commerce automation platform over the years.

They’ve got a ton of positive reviews, earning a 4.58 out of 5 rating on business software review site Software Advice. 

Let me start by talking about their drag-and-drop system for crafting awesome emails. It’s easy to use and allows for a high level of customization. And every template that Campaign Monitor uses is fully responsive, so you know your email will look great on any device. 

They offer a huge archive of free templates you can use for all types of emails, including newsletters, announcements, deals and offers, events, feedback, and more.

They have several automated emails that allow you to send targeted content to subscribers at the perfect times.

Some examples include welcome emails, reminders, birthday offers and VIP offers.

There’s also a re-engagement series that increases the likelihood of making repeat sales and establishing deeper brand loyalty.

And as you’re probably well aware, personalization has pretty much become a must for email marketers as of late.

Another thing I love about Campaign Monitor is that they have a built-in framework for personalization by allowing you to build smart segments. For instance, you can store custom data like location, interests, or gender to make your content hyper-relevant and maximize engagement.

They also feature segmentation so you can target exactly the right audience at the right time, helping your email campaign go farther. 

There’s a lot to love about Campaign Monitor, and it’s certainly a platform worth considering, especially if personalization is one of your main goals. 

4. AWeber 

“Powerfully-simple email marketing software” is AWeber’s UVP.

And it’s fitting considering how user-friendly of a platform it is. 

One of the most notable features is the smart designer, which is an “AI-powered email template designer that automatically creates amazing-looking branded emails in just seconds.”

If you’re just getting your feet wet with email marketing and aren’t sure how to get started, this can be super helpful for learning the ropes.

Like most other quality email marketing platforms, AWeber offers a free template library where you can quickly choose from a variety of pre-built themes.

There’s also drag and drop email creation, which allows you to create fully customized emails with zero coding knowledge.

Aweber has a solid automation system that allows you to assign triggers so that you send targeted emails at the perfect moment.

There’s robust analytics in place so you can continually capture data to improve the decision-making of your campaign.

I also like how AWeber makes it so easy to manage your list of subscribers for streamlined segmentation and targeting. 

As I mentioned before, personalization is vital for boosting engagement, and this feature makes it a breeze for beginners.

And the fact that this platform integrates so easily with many other systems like Shopify, WordPress, and Facebook is definitely appealing. 

There’s nothing fancy about AWeber, but it’s a rock-solid email marketing platform that offers all of the features you need and is a great choice for beginners. 

5. Sleeknote

Although Sleeknote isn’t a full-scale email marketing platform in the conventional sense, it has some amazing features that are super helpful for growing your email list.

It places emphasis on converting targeted website visitors into email leads without hurting the user experience. There are a few main ways Sleeknote helps you go about that.

First, it enables you to create branded on-page messages, which are like lead capture forms on steroids.

It’s all about creating aesthetically beautiful forms that naturally grab a visitor’s attention and piques their interest in your offer. 

Sleeknote allows you to fully design and customize these forms, add or remove input fields, and choose your unique preferences.

Next, there are multiple list building tools that can help you grow your email list more efficiently. 

For instance, advanced page-specific targeting lets you display on-page messages on the optimal page where a visitor will likely have the most interest. 

There are two-step optins, where you can create high-converting content upgrades by bringing elements like a button, link, or image into a two-step optin form. 

Sleeknote also ensures that your lead capture forms are compliant with Google’s guidelines so that you don’t violate their interstitials policy. 

Not only does this keep your e-commerce brand on Google’s good side for better search visibility, but it also contributes to creating the best possible visitor experience and maximizing conversion rates. 

And when you generate email leads, Sleeknote can automatically place them in your favorite email service provider for you. An added plus is that you have access to detailed analytics, so you can track the performance of your campaign at all times.

So, if you’re looking to get the absolute most from your lead capture forms, grow your email list more quickly and increase engagement, Sleeknote is certainly a platform to check out. 

6. SendInBlue

SendInBlue is a SaaS company that offers several different services like CRM, chat and, SMS marketing. They also happen to have an outstanding email marketing platform that I love, mainly because of the simplicity factor. 

There’s an awesome drag-and-drop editor that allows you to do all of the essentials. 

With it, you can:

  • Choose from a nice variety of pre-made templates (or build one from scratch if you’d like)
  • Create emails based on a style that perfectly jives with your brand identity
  • Personalize your email using your brand logo, beautiful images, your contact’s name, and more

SendInBlue understands the importance of giving each subscriber a unique experience, so you can easily segment your list based on different criteria, such as geography, gender, purchase history, etc.

They also implement cutting-edge machine learning technology to continually perform A/B testing and optimize your email content.

SendInBlue can even aggregate data to help you figure out what the best send time is. 

That way, you can send out emails at a targeted time to maximize your open rate and overall engagement. 

So, when you put it all together, SendInBlue is an incredibly innovative platform that can help you elevate your email marketing to the next level. 

7. GetResponse

Similar to SendInBlue, this final platform also provides multiple services like marketing automation, landing pages, and webinars. But it also offers top-notch email marketing that covers all of the bases.

In their own words, “GetResponse offers a suite of email marketing tools designed to deliver tailor-made offers to your contacts when they’re most active.”

You have access to hundreds of professional-looking templates, as well as over 5,000 licensed images from Shutterstock. That way, you don’t have to worry about finding your own photos. 

You can create ultra-personalized messages by using subscriber information, such as purchase history, where they’re at in the subscription lifecycle, geography, and so on. 

GetResponse makes it a breeze to perform split testing on subject lines, images and send times to fully optimize them.

They also allow you to sync up with other tools like PayPal, WordPress, and Instagram to “unite subscriber and customer details.”

One of the added perks of choosing this GetResponse is that they give you access to several webinars where you can learn the ins and outs of the platform so you can take full advantage of all of the features. 

And with a proven 99 percent deliverability rate, it’s hard to argue against GetResponse.
